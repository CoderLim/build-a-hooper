## What “build your hooper” actually means

When you **build your hooper**, you are not picking a finished card. You are stacking decisions under incomplete information:

1. Pick a mode
2. Spin skills tied to legendary or modern team seasons
3. Lock one attribute per round from real roster grades
4. Publish the player, join a career team, simulate the season

That sequence is the whole job. Theory helps after a few ugly cards. First runs should show what breaks — not chase a perfect sheet.

Short version: to **build your hooper** well, commit to one identity per run, then let the season say whether it was real. Skip that and you reroll noise instead of learning.

## Modes change how you build your hooper

Three modes sit on the start screen. They do not rewrite the 13 attributes. They change how much you see while you **build your hooper**.

- **Classic** — pick position, see grades, three rerolls. Best place to learn how attributes stack.
- **Blind** — position stays visible; grades hide. You commit earlier.
- **Chaos** — position and grades stay dark until the end. Builds that need full information fall apart here.

A Classic sniper does not auto-transfer to Chaos. When you **build your hooper** in Blind or Chaos, plan for missing pieces. Keep a second skill path if the first spin refuses. Mode badges and ring ladders on [/achievements](/achievements) lean harder on Blind and Chaos — that is where the draft hurts.

New to **build your hooper**? Classic until you can name which attributes support which role. Then Blind. Chaos after you stop panic-rerolling.

## The 13 attributes when you build your hooper

The sheet is the same every run: **3PT / MID / FIN / DNK / HAN / PAS / PDEF / IDEF / BLK / REB / ATH / STR / CLU**.

Treat those as clusters when you **build your hooper**, not thirteen separate upgrades:

- **Scoring** — 3PT, MID, FIN, DNK
- **Creation** — HAN, PAS
- **Defense / glass** — PDEF, IDEF, BLK, REB
- **Physical / close** — ATH, STR, CLU

Strong cards reinforce one cluster and borrow from a second. A pure shooter with empty handles dies late. A wall with no finishing stalls on offense. Perfect Balance and GOAT Blueprint exist because people max everything; seasons punish that greed when you **build your hooper**.

After three locks, say the role out loud — “floor general,” “paint bully,” “two-way wing.” Cannot name it? You are collecting grades, not trying to **build your hooper**. That check saves more drafts than any tip sheet.

## Season logic after you build your hooper

Publishing is not the finish line. The 82-game schedule is. Peak nights look loud on a clip; average nights decide whether the card holds after you **build your hooper**.

Ask these every time you **build your hooper** and then sim:

- Does this player still make sense in game 40 and game 60?
- Can the build survive a cold shooting week?
- Which matchup exposes the hole you ignored at draft?

Consistency beats a one-week explosion. Long seasons feel different from arcade “drop 50 and leave.” Optimize only the first ten games and you did not really **build your hooper** — you built a demo.

## Common mistakes when you build your hooper

- Chasing A+ everywhere instead of a readable role
- Treating Classic success as proof the same sheet works in Chaos

- Ignoring handles or passing on “pure scorer” runs
- Forgetting Blind/Chaos ring ladders stay empty if you only live in Classic
