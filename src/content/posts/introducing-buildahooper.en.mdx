**Build a Hooper** is an unofficial browser basketball simulator where you draft skills inspired by legendary and modern teams, shape a custom player across **13 attributes**, and push through a full **82-game season** — all without installing anything.

If you have heard the name from search, a friend, or social media and want to know what the game actually is, this article is a readable starting point before your first draft.

## What is Build a Hooper?

Build a Hooper turns roster-building fantasy into a fast, replayable simulation loop. Instead of watching one highlight play, you are testing a theory: _what kind of player actually wins over time?_

The core loop has three beats:

1. **Draft a direction** — choose skills that point toward a clear identity, not a pile of disconnected bonuses.
2. **Build around attributes** — with 13 attributes in play, synergy matters more than one isolated number.
3. **Survive the season** — an 82-game schedule rewards consistency, not just peak nights.

Every run is a small basketball experiment. The fun starts at the draft board, but the depth shows up when you realize each skill choice changes how you should value attributes — and how much risk your season can tolerate.

## Three modes, three puzzles

Build a Hooper ships with **three modes** — Classic, Blind, and Chaos — each changing what you see during the build and how much you can plan ahead.

- **Classic** — the most readable starting point. You see what you are drafting and can plan around a clear identity.
- **Blind** — hides more information during the build, pushing you to commit to a direction earlier.
- **Chaos** — the wildest environment. Great for testing whether a build idea holds up when conditions get unpredictable.

Do not assume one successful build transfers perfectly across modes. Treat each mode as a new basketball environment and adjust your thinking accordingly.

## How attributes shape your build

With 13 attributes on the board, no single stat carries everything. The strongest runs usually come from builds where several strengths reinforce each other.

If your draft leans toward explosive offense, you still need enough balance to avoid collapsing during a long season. If your choices favor all-around stability, the goal becomes maximizing repeatable value rather than chasing the most dramatic peak.

Think about finishing, creation, defense, physical tools, and decision-making as parts of one identity — not as isolated upgrades. When two or three attributes support the same role, your player becomes easier to read and easier to improve across multiple runs.

## Why the 82-game season matters

The season structure is central to the fantasy. A long schedule shifts the goal from momentary excitement to repeatable performance.

Strong season builds usually have a plan for average nights, not just peak nights. Ask whether your player still makes sense in game 40, game 60, and game 82. Can the build recover from a cold stretch? Does it have enough balance to survive matchups that expose its weakness?

The best players in Build a Hooper are often not the most dramatic — they are the ones whose strengths still matter late in the schedule.

## Unofficial status

Build a Hooper is an **unofficial basketball simulator**. It is not affiliated with the NBA, any official league, team, logo, photo, or uniform rights holder.

That independence is part of the point: this is a sandbox for basketball theory, built for players who enjoy experimenting with roster logic in the browser. You can play it online at [buildahooper.org](https://buildahooper.org).
